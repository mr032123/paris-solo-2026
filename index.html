How to read this  Everything here is walk-in, browsable, and priced — no appointments, no white-glove galleries where you're the only one in the room. Days mix design, fashion, jewelry, and trims so nothing is single-minded. Rugs are French/Deco only (the Puces is the place). August note  Mid-August is fermeture annuelle season — department stores, the Puces, concept stores, and mercerie stay open; a few smaller shops may be shut, so a quick call ahead never hurts. Aug 15 is a Saturday holiday (the Puces run anyway).

Day 1

August 12

Wednesday · Left Bank

Curiosities, hardware, antiquaires, and the great Left Bank department store — a design day that stays browsable.

Rue du Bac & Saint-Germain

Deyrolle

Pop-in46 rue du Bac, 7th

The 1831 cabinet of curiosities — taxidermy, minerals, butterflies, and the reissued 19th-c natural-history prints you can actually buy and frame. Pure inspiration, no pressure.

Map46 rue du Bac

La Quincaillerie

Pop-inBd Saint-Germain, 5th

The decorative-hardware destination: knobs, pulls, handles, hooks — including pieces cast from Gio Ponti, Jean Nouvel and Jasper Morrison molds. Genuinely useful for the apartment; all priced, dig freely.

Map3–15 Bd Saint-Germain

Rue de Seine windows & the Carré Rive Gauche

Stroll6th / 7th

Walk the collectible-design and antiquaire windows (rue de Seine, rue de Lille, rue de Beaune) for inspiration — peek in, no obligation to engage. Preclothed (3 rue Bonaparte) is the browsable vintage-fashion stop here.

Maprue de Seine · Carré Rive Gauche

Le Bon Marché (the solo grand-magasin)

Open in August24 rue de Sèvres, 7th

If you don't finish the department stores with Sara, do the Left Bank one solo here — home floor, La Grande Épicerie next door, and the calmest of the grands magasins.

Map24 rue de Sèvres

Lunch · salad + good bread  Cuisine de Bar by Poilâne (38 rue du Cherche-Midi) — tartines and salads on the legendary Poilâne bread; or Le Bar du Marché (rue de Seine/Buci) — buzzy café that does a proper salade niçoise.
Dinner · fish, convivial  La Boissonnerie (69 rue de Seine) — warm room, strong fish menu, easy solo at a small table.

Day 2

August 13

Thursday · Montmartre → Haussmann → 1st/2nd

Start high in Montmartre for fabric and brocante, drift down through the grands magasins, and finish among the trimming houses near the hotel.

Morning · Montmartre

Marché Saint-Pierre

Pop-in18th, Anvers

The Montmartre fabric quarter — you flagged their designer deadstock. Multi-floor Dreyfus plus the coupon shops nearby; go for the special end-of-roll piece, not a full haul.

Sacré Coupons

Pop-in4 bis rue d'Orsel, 18th

A coupon/remnant shop in the Montmartre fabric district — end-of-roll cuts and offcuts, with the occasional couture-house end among the general stock. Worth a rummage while you're up here.

Map4 bis rue d'Orsel

L'Objet Qui Parle

Pop-inrue des Martyrs, 18th

A cabinet-of-curiosities brocante on the way down from Abbesses — small decorative objects to dig through. Galerie Pénélope nearby for antique/Deco jewelry.

Day 3

August 14

Friday · East side — Marais → 10th → 11th

The big mixed browse: concept stores, lighting, objects, trims, a design warehouse, and the accessible vintage-home shops — all contiguous, pick as many as you like.

Marais · objects, lighting, trims

Merci

Pop-inBd Beaumarchais, 3rd

The concept store — floors of furniture, lighting, textiles and objects, all priced, zero pressure, plus the café-library for a pause. Fleux (rue Ste-Croix de la Bretonnerie) is the other big browsable design shop nearby.

Map111 Bd Beaumarchais

Empreintes

Pop-inrue de Picardie, 3rd

The Ateliers d'Art de France concept store — ~1,000 French craft pieces across ceramics, glass, lighting, jewelry and objects, with a real range of prices. Best single stop for accessories. Sentou (rue François Miron) adds design lighting and objects.

Map5 rue de Picardie · Sentou

Entrée des Fournisseurs

Pop-inrue des Francs-Bourgeois, 3rd

An Aladdin's-cave haberdashery at the back of a courtyard — ribbons, buttons, trims. Open Saturdays too. Houlès (rue Saint-Nicolas, toward Bastille) is the passementerie + curtain-hardware house.

Map8 rue des Francs-Bourgeois · Houlès

Malhia Kent — couture tweeds

Pop-inViaduc des Arts, 12th

The high-end fabric stop: the atelier-boutique that's woven couture tweeds and brocades for Chanel since 1960, under the arches of the Viaduc des Arts (toward Bastille, near Houlès). New collections each season plus swatches and end-of-line — the one fabric detour worth making. Dressmaking/couture weight, not upholstery.

Map19 av. Daumesnil

Demain Rétro + Haut-Marais fashion

Pop-inMarais, 3rd

A mixed concept store — vintage fashion, jewelry, and decor in one — plus your Haut-Marais vintage-fashion pins from the family list are all right here (The Broken Arm, At Dawn, and the rest).

MapDemain Rétro

Lunch · market salads & bread  Marché des Enfants Rouges (39 rue de Bretagne) — Paris's oldest covered market; grab a big salad and good bread from the stalls, right between Merci and Empreintes.
Push east · a warehouse + vintage home

Galerie Paradis

Pop-inrue de Paradis, 10th

Your kind of place, not the intimidating kind: a 1,000 m² former ceramics factory of vintage + contemporary design and "popular-art" objects, 70+ makers, eclectic and open to wander. The anti-white-glove gallery.

Maprue de Paradis

Belle Lurette & Carouche — vintage home (11th)

Pop-inCharonne / Popincourt, 11th

The "click under gallery" tier: mirrors, lighting, and furniture from the 1890s–70s, restocked weekly, browse-and-leave. Carouche does hunted-and-restored pieces a few streets on.

Map5 rue du Marché Popincourt

Dinner · in the current  Septime La Cave (3 rue Basfroi) for a light standing graze, or Le Baratin (Belleville) for a beloved bistro table.
West (Marais) to east (11th) — browse as far as your feet last.
Day 4

August 15

Saturday · The Puces (Assumption — open)

The high-and-low motherlode, and the un-intimidating way to see gallery-grade design: open market, priced, browse for hours, talk to no one. This is where the French/Deco rugs are.

Puces de Saint-Ouen · go early

Paul Bert–Serpette

Open Satrue des Rosiers, Saint-Ouen

The design heart of the market — Art Deco and 20th-c furniture, lighting, and French/Deco rugs (Da Silva Bruhns–style carpets turn up here). L'Atelier 55 has a stand here too (vintage French & Italian '50s–'80s).

Map110 rue des Rosiers

Vernaison

Open SatSaint-Ouen

The rambling, atmospheric market — vintage passementerie, trims, hardware, curios, and textiles. The place for the odd, characterful small find.

Map99 rue des Rosiers

Biron

Open SatSaint-Ouen

The polished market for finer antiques and estate jewelry — a calmer browse to close the loop.

Map85 rue des Rosiers

Lunch · on site  Ma Cocotte (106 rue des Rosiers) — the Starck-designed market brasserie; salads and a proper lunch to refuel between markets.
Dinner · the scene finale  Caviar Kaspia (17 Place de la Madeleine) — the see-and-be-seen fish-and-caviar room, one of the great tables to sit alone and watch Paris. A fitting last solo night.
The markets cluster on rue des Rosiers, Saint-Ouen (north); Kaspia is back by the hotel.
Anytime

Culture & the 8th

Open in August · slot into any gap

Reliable culture (museums stay open in August) plus a cluster in the 8th you can pair with the Kaspia dinner or a quiet morning. Note: Drouot and the Beaux-Arts are on summer recess mid-August — don't build around them.

Worth the detour

Musée des Arts Décoratifs (MAD)

Open, near hotel107 rue de Rivoli, 1st

Go for the permanent Art Deco & decorative-arts galleries — your exact world. (Skip the current temporary show, Royal Thai Dress — not your thing.) Steps from the hotel; ideal for a hot afternoon.

Map107 rue de Rivoli

Hôtel de la Marine

Open dailyPlace de la Concorde, 8th

The restored 18th-century mansion on Concorde — the intendant's gilded apartments recreated to the period, plus the Al Thani Collection of decorative arts. A jewel-box interior, exactly your world, steps from the hotel.

Map2 Place de la Concorde

Fondation Cartier (optional · contemporary)

Tue till 10pm2 Place du Palais-Royal, 1st

Newly moved (Oct 2025) into a Jean Nouvel building opposite the Louvre — five minutes from the hotel. The 40-years-of-the-collection show runs through Aug 23. Contemporary rather than decorative, so optional — but the building's worth it, and the Tuesday late night pairs with your Palais-Royal evening.

Map2 Place du Palais-Royal

Musée Jacquemart-André

Open daily158 Bd Haussmann, 8th

A Haussmann-era mansion of decorative arts and paintings — gold-leaf ceilings, monumental fireplaces, double-height galleries — freshly reopened after a full restoration. An intimate, deeply collected interior, exactly your world. (In the Marais, Musée Cognacq-Jay is the 18th-c-decorative-arts alternative, open Tue–Sun.)

Map158 Bd Haussmann

Benneton Graveur (stationery, grander alt)

Order + ship75 Bd Malesherbes, 8th

The other classic engraver since 1880, if you want a second option to Papier Royal — near Jacquemart-André, so pair them. Custom is made-to-order; have it shipped.

Map75 Bd Malesherbes

Galerie Marcilhac (Deco reference — window only)

Inspirationrue du Fbg Saint-Honoré, 8th

The Art Deco reference gallery (Da Silva Bruhns, the great ensembliers). It's a proper high-end gallery, so treat it as a window/inspiration stop, not a casual buy — and it may be closed mid-August. Worth a look if you're passing to Kaspia.

Map120 rue du Fbg Saint-Honoré
